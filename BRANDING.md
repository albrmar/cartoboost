# CartoBoost Branding

CartoBoost uses a spatial-cell route mark to represent structured boosting for place, time, and movement.

## Concept

The primary mark combines:

- a hexagonal cell for H3-style spatial indexing and route-cell membership;
- an origin-destination route for directed movement structure;
- an upward gradient arrow for boosting, lift, and forecasting improvement.

This avoids generic GIS branding. CartoBoost should look like a modeling toolkit, not a map application.

## Assets

- `assets/brand/cartoboost-logo.svg` — primary README/docs lockup.
- `assets/brand/cartoboost-mark.svg` — compact icon for favicon, package card, or social avatar.
- `assets/brand/cartoboost-logo-concepts.svg` — concept board for alternate directions.

| Lift violet | `#7C3AED` | Arrowhead and boost terminus |
| Cell blue | `#E0F2FE` | Spatial cell fill |
| Off white | `#F8FAFC` | Background |

## Tagline

Preferred tagline:

> Structured boosting for place, time, and movement.

More technical alternate:

> Geotemporal boosting for regression, forecasting, and route-structured data.

## Usage rules

Use the full lockup on README, docs landing pages, and release artifacts. Use the compact mark when the logo needs to survive at small sizes. Do not replace the mark with generic globe, map-pin, rocket, brain, or bar-chart imagery.
